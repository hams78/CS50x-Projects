Provide designations for sort1, sort2, and sort3.
Explain your rationale for each designation.

sort1 uses: bubble sort
How do you know?
sort1 is exceptionally fast on sorted lists, achieving nearly linear time, but its runtime scales quadratically on reversed datasets due to the extensive number of adjacent swaps required.

sort2 uses: merge sort
How do you know?
sort2 handles the largest datasets with the least amount of time scaling. Its divide-and-conquer approach allows it to maintain an O(n log n) efficiency, outperforming the others on randomized and reversed lists.

sort3 uses: selection sort
How do you know?
sort3 takes a consistent amount of time across sorted, randomized, and reversed inputs because it always scans the entire remaining unsorted array to find the minimum element, showing O(n^2) behavior in all cases.
